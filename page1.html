The History of Cryptography

The history of cryptography can be traced back to ancient times, where it was used for military and diplomatic purposes. One of the most famous examples of early cryptography is the Caesar cipher, which was used by Julius Caesar to communicate with his generals.

Modern Cryptography

With the advent of computers and the internet, cryptography has become more important than ever. Modern cryptography includes algorithms such as AES, RSA, and ECC, which are used to secure communication and protect sensitive data.

Symmetric Cryptography Asymmetric Cryptography
Uses the same key for encryption and decryption Uses a public key for encryption and a private key for decryption
Fast and efficient Slower but more secure
Examples: AES, DES, 3DES Examples: RSA, ECC, DSA
